Abstract

Recently, text-to-video diffusion models have shown remarkable success in generating a single video clip from a single prompt. Building on this advancement, several works have extended these models to generate long videos with multiple prompts, enabling dynamic changes controlled by each prompt. However, these approaches often focus solely on smooth transitions between adjacent video clips, leading to a drift of content or semantics across the entire video due to a lack of global synchronization. In this work, we propose Synchronized Coupled Sampling (SynCoS), a new sampling framework that extends any text-to-video diffusion model by synchronizing denoising across multiple video chunks beyond adjacent clips. To achieve this, we couple DDIM with optimization-based sampling to facilitate synchronous integration across multiple video chunks. Experimental results demonstrate that our method generates long videos with diverse changes controlled by each prompt while ensuring seamless transitions between adjacent chunks and long-term consistency throughout the entire video.

Overview


This is an overview of our tuning-free sampling framework, SynCoS. Mainly, it consists of three main stages.
1. Perform a DDIM update for each overlapping short video chunk. At this stage, instead of fully reverting each chunk to its previous timestep, we proceed until obtaining the data space samples through DDIM update, then fuse them to ensure smooth transitions between overlapping chunks.
2. Refine the fused output using CSD. By intervening in the DDIM update and conceptualizing them as a refinement task, SynCoS effectively achieves global synchronization across multiple video chunks.
3. Resume the DDIM update to revert the refined, fused chunks to the previous timestep.
